2 Kings 22 recounts the discovery of the Book of the Law in the temple and King Josiah’s response, marking the beginning of a significant spiritual reform. Read the full passage {{ "here" | hyperlink: "https://www.bibliaonline.com.br/niv/2rs/22", true }}.

---

## Text Structure

### 2 Kgs 22:1-7

Josiah’s **reign**. Josiah leads reforms and seeks to restore the temple, demonstrating commitment to order and care for the house of God.

### 2 Kgs 22:8-10

The **discovery of the Book of the Law**. During restoration, the Book of the Law is found and read, bringing forgotten instructions back to light.

### 2 Kgs 22:11-13

Josiah’s **response**. Upon hearing the words, Josiah humbles himself and recognizes the people’s departure from God, revealing spiritual sensitivity.

### 2 Kgs 22:14-20

The **prophetic inquiry**. God’s word confirms judgment, but also acknowledges Josiah’s humility, bringing a balanced response.

## Reflection

2 Kings 22 shows that returning to the Word can transform realities. The rediscovery brought direction and awareness.

Josiah’s attitude reveals the importance of a sensitive heart, willing to acknowledge mistakes and seek change.

As presented in **Prophets and Kings**, chapter 32, true reform begins with the Word and with a sincere response before God.
