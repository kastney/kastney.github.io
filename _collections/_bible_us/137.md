2 Kings 5 recounts Naaman's healing and the lessons on humility, obedience, and integrity, highlighting God's work in different hearts. Read the full passage {{ "here" | hyperlink: "https://www.bibliaonline.com.br/niv/2rs/5", true }}.

---

## Text Structure

### 2 Kgs 5:1-7

**Naaman's need.** Even as an important leader, Naaman suffers from leprosy, showing that status does not eliminate deep personal needs.

### 2 Kgs 5:8-12

**The initial resistance.** Naaman rejects the simplicity of the instruction, demonstrating difficulty in accepting a path that does not match his expectations.

### 2 Kgs 5:13-14

**The decision to obey.** After receiving guidance, Naaman submits and is healed, revealing that obedience is essential to experience God's work.

### 2 Kgs 5:15-19

**The acknowledgment of God.** Naaman declares his faith and seeks to live differently, demonstrating an inner transformation.

### 2 Kgs 5:20-24

**Gehazi's greed.** Gehazi seeks personal gain, going against Elisha's example and revealing a heart out of alignment.

### 2 Kgs 5:25-27

**The consequence of wrongdoing.** Gehazi is confronted and suffers the consequences of his actions, showing that God also deals with a lack of integrity.

## Reflection

2 Kings 5 shows that God's blessing is often connected to humility. Naaman had to set aside his pride in order to experience healing.

The simplicity of the path reveals that God's way of working does not always align with human expectations.

As presented in **Prophets and Kings**, chapter 20, true transformation involves not only receiving blessings but also aligning one's character with God's will.
