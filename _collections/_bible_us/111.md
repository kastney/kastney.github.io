1 Samuel 26 recounts when David once again spares Saul’s life, showing that his previous decision was not momentary, but the result of a firm character. Read the full text {{ "here" | hyperlink: "https://www.bibliaonline.com.br/niv/1sm/26", true }}.

## Text Structure

### 1Sm 26:1-5

The **renewed pursuit**. Saul resumes his pursuit of David, showing that his previous change was not lasting, while David observes the situation and plans his actions carefully.

### 1Sm 26:6-12

The **repeated opportunity**. David enters Saul’s camp at night, once again having the chance to kill him, but decides only to take the spear and the jug as evidence.

### 1Sm 26:13-16

The **public exposure**. David positions himself at a distance and reveals what happened, showing that he spared Saul once more, highlighting his integrity.

### 1Sm 26:17-20

David’s **declaration**. He reaffirms his innocence and trust in God, refusing to act out of revenge and maintaining his righteous stance.

### 1Sm 26:21-25

Saul’s **response**. Saul again acknowledges his error and blesses David, admitting that he would succeed, even though his attitude does not endure.

## Reflection

1 Samuel 26 shows that true character is revealed in repetition. David had another opportunity and made the same right choice.

David’s consistency reveals spiritual maturity. He was not guided by the emotion of the moment, but by firm principles.

As presented in **Patriarchs and Prophets**, chapter 65, true greatness lies in trusting God and acting with integrity, even when it would be easier to act differently.
