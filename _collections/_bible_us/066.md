Numbers 16 recounts the rebellion of Korah, Dathan, and Abiram against Moses’ leadership, revealing the consequences of opposing the authority established by God. Read the full text {{ "here" | hyperlink: "https://www.bibliaonline.com.br/niv/nm/16", true }}.

---

## Text Structure

### Num 16:1-3

The **organized rebellion**. Korah and his followers question Moses and Aaron, claiming equality, but revealing dissatisfaction and ambition for leadership.

### Num 16:4-7

The **response of Moses**. He falls facedown before God and proposes a test, showing that the final decision about leadership belongs to the Lord.

### Num 16:8-11

The **direct confrontation**. Moses exposes the true motivation of the group: it was not justice, but a desire to take positions that God had not given.

### Num 16:12-15

The **open resistance**. Dathan and Abiram refuse to appear, unjustly accusing Moses and distorting the facts to justify their rebellion.

### Num 16:16-19

The **preparation for judgment**. Those involved present themselves before the Lord, and God’s glory appears before the whole assembly.

### Num 16:20-24

The **divine warning**. God commands the people to move away from the rebels, separating those who would be spared from judgment.

### Num 16:25-35

The **judgment of God**. The earth opens and swallows the leaders of the rebellion, while fire consumes others, demonstrating divine authority.

### Num 16:36-40

The **remembrance of the event**. The censers are turned into a lasting sign, reminding of the consequences of rebellion.

### Num 16:41-50

The **new crisis and intercession**. The people complain again, and a plague begins, which is stopped by Aaron’s intercession.

## Reflection

Numbers 16 shows that not every claim of equality is just. Sometimes, behind the argument, there is dissatisfaction and a desire for position.

Moses’ posture reveals dependence on God. Instead of reacting with his own strength, he entrusts the situation to the Lord and trusts in His justice.

The rebellion demonstrates how the human heart can distort reality, turning deliverance into accusation and leadership into oppression.

This passage teaches that God takes seriously the order He establishes. Resisting it is not just a human conflict, but a spiritual matter.
