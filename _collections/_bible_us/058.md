## Text Structure

### Ex 34:1-4

A **new opportunity**. God instructs Moses to prepare new tablets, indicating that even after failure, there is room for a fresh start.

### Ex 34:5-7

The **revelation of God’s character**. God reveals Himself as merciful, just, and faithful, showing a balance between grace and responsibility.

### Ex 34:8-9

Moses’ **response**. He bows in worship and intercedes for the people, recognizing the ongoing need for God’s presence.

### Ex 34:10-14

The **renewal of the covenant**. God reaffirms His promises and calls the people to faithfulness, warning against idolatry.

### Ex 34:29-30

The **reflected glory**. Moses’ face shines after being with God, demonstrating the impact of the divine presence.

### Ex 34:31-35

The **ongoing relationship**. Moses maintains fellowship with God, reflecting His glory before the people and showing continual transformation.

## Reflection

Exodus 34 shows that failure does not end the story when there is repentance. God offers new beginnings, but also calls for responsibility and faithfulness.

The revelation of God’s character is central: He is merciful, but also just. He forgives, yet does not ignore sin, maintaining perfect balance.

As presented in **Patriarchs and Prophets**, chapter 28, the renewal of the covenant demonstrates that God desires to restore the relationship, even after deep failure.

This passage teaches that God’s presence transforms. The closer we are to Him, the more we reflect His character in our actions and daily life.
