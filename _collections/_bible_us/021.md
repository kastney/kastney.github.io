## Text Structure

### Gen 24:1-9

The **entrusted mission**. Abraham sends his servant with an important responsibility, instructing him to find a suitable wife for Isaac.

### Gen 24:10-14

The **prayer for guidance**. The servant asks God for a specific sign, demonstrating total dependence before taking action.

### Gen 24:15-21

The **immediate answer**. Rebekah appears and fulfills exactly the sign requested, revealing divine providence in action.

### Gen 24:22-27

The **recognition of God**. The servant worships the Lord, acknowledging that God guided every step of the journey.

### Gen 24:28-33

The **reception at the house**. Rebekah brings the servant to her family, and he remains focused on the mission before accepting hospitality.

### Gen 24:34-46

The **account of divine guidance**. The servant recounts the entire experience, showing how God led every detail.

### Gen 24:48

The **final worship**. The servant bows in gratitude, recognizing that God led him exactly to the right place.

## Reflection

Genesis 24 shows that God cares about the details of life. From the sending of the servant to the meeting with Rebekah, everything happens in a guided and intentional way.

The servant’s prayer reveals a practical faith. He not only asks for guidance but also carefully observes the answer, showing sensitivity to God’s action.

Divine providence is revealed at the right time. Even before the prayer is finished, the answer was already on the way, showing that God works beyond what we can perceive.

This passage teaches us that trusting God involves seeking guidance, acting faithfully, and recognizing His hand in every step of the journey.
