1 Samuel 12 presents Samuel’s speech to the people, reaffirming God’s faithfulness and calling Israel to spiritual responsibility. Read the full text {{ "here" | hyperlink: "https://www.bibliaonline.com.br/niv/1sm/12", true }}.

---

## Text Structure

### 1Sm 12:1-5

The **integrity of Samuel**. Samuel presents his life before the people and confirms his righteousness, showing that his leadership was marked by transparency and faithfulness over time.

### 1Sm 12:6-11

The **remembrance of history**. Samuel recalls how God delivered and sustained Israel, showing that divine faithfulness has always been present throughout the people’s journey.

### 1Sm 12:12-15

The **people’s mistake**. The request for a king is exposed as a rejection of God, yet there is still an opportunity to live in obedience and experience blessings.

### 1Sm 12:16-18

The **supernatural sign**. God sends thunder and rain out of season, confirming the seriousness of the message and awakening fear in the people.

### 1Sm 12:19-22

The **repentance and hope**. The people recognize their error and ask for intercession, and Samuel reaffirms that God does not abandon those who seek Him.

### 1Sm 12:23-25

The **final commitment**. Samuel promises to continue praying and instructing, while reinforcing the importance of serving God with sincerity.

## Reflection

1 Samuel 12 shows that God’s faithfulness remains even when people fail. He continues to offer opportunities for return and restoration.

Samuel’s speech reveals that acknowledging mistakes is an essential part of the relationship with God. Repentance opens the way for new beginnings.

As presented in **Patriarchs and Prophets**, chapter 59, God does not reject those who turn to Him, but desires sincere commitment.

This passage teaches that serving God involves continual decision. It is not enough to start well; it is necessary to remain with integrity.
