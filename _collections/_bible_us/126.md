## Text Structure

### 1 Kgs 10:1-5

The **visit of the Queen of Sheba**. The queen arrives to test Solomon with difficult questions and is impressed by his wisdom, organization, and wealth, recognizing something extraordinary.

### 1 Kgs 10:6-9

The **public recognition**. She declares that the reports were true and even greater than she had heard, attributing the wisdom given to Solomon to God.

### 1 Kgs 10:10-13

The **exchange of gifts**. The queen offers riches and receives generosity from Solomon, demonstrating honor and reciprocity between leaders.

### 1 Kgs 10:14-22

The **accumulated wealth**. The text describes the abundance of gold and goods, highlighting the prosperity of the kingdom.

### 1 Kgs 10:23-25

The **international fame**. Kings from all over the earth seek to hear Solomon’s wisdom, expanding his influence.

### 1 Kgs 10:26-29

The **military and economic structure**. Solomon strengthens his kingdom with resources and organization, consolidating his power.

## Reflection

1 Kings 10 shows that God’s wisdom can impact beyond the local context, reaching other nations and people.

Solomon’s prosperity reveals the result of a reign initially aligned with God, reflecting order and excellence.

As presented in **Prophets and Kings**, chapter 3, success also brings risks, requiring vigilance so that the heart remains on the right path.
