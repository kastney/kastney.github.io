2 Samuel 15 recounts Absalom’s rebellion against David and the king’s flight, revealing pain, consequences, and trust in God in the midst of crisis. Read the full text {{ "here" | hyperlink: "https://www.bibliaonline.com.br/niv/2sm/15", true }}.

---

## Text Structure

### 2Sm 15:1-6

**Absalom’s strategy**. Absalom wins the hearts of the people through closeness and false promises, gradually undermining trust in David and preparing the ground for his revolt.

### 2Sm 15:7-12

**The declaration of rebellion**. Absalom proclaims himself king in Hebron, consolidating support and initiating a conspiracy that quickly grows in influence.

### 2Sm 15:13-17

**David’s flight**. Upon hearing of the conspiracy, David decides to leave Jerusalem, avoiding direct confrontation and protecting the people from greater conflict.

### 2Sm 15:18-23

**Loyalty in the midst of crisis**. Some remain faithful to David, demonstrating commitment even in the face of instability and risk.

### 2Sm 15:24-29

**Trust in God**. David sends the ark back to Jerusalem, showing that his trust is not in symbols, but in God’s will.

### 2Sm 15:30-37

**Pain and dependence**. David ascends the hill weeping, yet seeks God and organizes support, showing faith even in the midst of sorrow.

## Reflection

2 Samuel 15 shows that consequences can arise within one’s own household. Absalom’s rebellion reveals a painful and unexpected scenario.

David’s attitude demonstrates humility and wisdom. He does not react impulsively, but seeks to preserve lives and trust in God.

This passage teaches that even in moments of loss and betrayal, it is possible to maintain faith and direction, trusting in God’s work.
