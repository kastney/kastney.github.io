2 Kings 20 recounts Hezekiah’s illness, his prayer, God’s response, and the consequences of his choices, revealing both grace and responsibility. Read the full passage {{ "here" | hyperlink: "https://www.bibliaonline.com.br/niv/2rs/20", true }}.

---

## Text Structure

### 2 Kgs 20:1-3

Hezekiah’s **prayer**. Faced with a declared death sentence, Hezekiah cries out to God with sincerity, revealing dependence and faith in a critical moment.

### 2 Kgs 20:4-7

God’s **response**. God hears the prayer, grants additional years of life, and provides instructions for healing, showing mercy and care.

### 2 Kgs 20:8-11

The **confirmed sign**. God offers a sign, reinforcing His word and strengthening Hezekiah’s trust.

### 2 Kgs 20:12-15

The **Babylonian visit**. Hezekiah receives envoys and shows them his treasures, revealing an attitude that would have future consequences.

### 2 Kgs 20:16-19

The **prophetic warning**. Isaiah announces that what was displayed would be taken away, pointing to the future consequences of his decisions.

### 2 Kgs 20:20-21

The **end of the reign**. The text concludes by highlighting Hezekiah’s achievements and his succession.

## Reflection

2 Kings 20 shows that God responds to sincere prayer, intervening even in critical situations.

Hezekiah’s experience reveals that received blessings require vigilance, since later decisions also carry consequences.

As presented in **Prophets and Kings**, chapter 28, spiritual life involves not only moments of faith but also ongoing responsibility in one’s choices.
